--- Raw model answer ---
The paper reports a single training step that took about **4.2 seconds** for the big model, with a total of roughly **300,000 steps** (about **12 hours**) on **8 P100 GPUs**.

More precisely from section 5.3:

- Base models: ~**100,000 steps** (~12 hours) on 8 P100 GPUs
- Big models: ~**300,000 steps** (~3.5 days) on 8 P100 GPUs
- Each training step of the big model took roughly **0.4 seconds** for the base, scaling up to about **4.2 seconds** for the big variant.

That wall-clock difference is the main reason the big model ends up much more expensive than the base despite identical architecture.
