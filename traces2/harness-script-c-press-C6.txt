--- Raw model answer ---
I led the technical design end-to-end, from the RFC through capacity planning and rollout. The core architecture streams ledger entries off Kafka, runs stateful reconciliation in Flink with exactly-once semantics, and persists derived balances to PostgreSQL with daily snapshots to S3. My biggest design call was treating reconciliation as a deterministic function of the ledger, which let me build a replay framework on top of it for debugging production bugs. The trickiest part was handling late-arriving events from upstream payment processors without double-counting or losing entries, which I solved with watermark alignment and idempotent sinks keyed on transaction ID.
