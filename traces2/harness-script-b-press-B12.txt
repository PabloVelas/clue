--- Raw model answer ---
The interviewer's question rests on a premise that isn't actually correct, so it's worth flagging that first.

In the Transformer base model, multi-head attention keeps total computational cost **approximately the same** as single-head attention because the per-head dimension is chosen so that head count × head dimension equals the original single-head dimension. So if a single head would project to 512 dimensions, 8 heads each project to 64 dimensions, giving 8 × 64 = 512. The total number of projection parameters and the total dot-product work per position is preserved. The point of splitting isn't saving FLOPs, it's giving each head a different **subspace** to attend in, so the model can jointly attend to information from different representation subspaces at different positions. The 8 × 64 split is the specific instantiation of that general design rule in the paper.

So the honest answer to the interviewer is: the cost is similar because $d_{\text{model}} = h \cdot d_k$ is held fixed, and the benefit isn't computational, it's representational.
