How I Would Investigate:

Reproduce the issue in a staging environment with the same load profile, then add distributed tracing across the producer, the topic, and the consumer to compare timestamps at each hop. Cross-check consumer offsets against producer commit logs, and inspect the Kafka broker logs for any ISR changes, rebalances, or out-of-memory events around the time of the incident.

Fix:

Switch the producer to acks=all with idempotent enabled, raise the consumer's isolation level to read_committed, and bump replication factor for the affected topic. Then rewind the consumer offset to the last known good position and replay the affected partitions.

Validation:

Run a chaos test that kills a broker mid-stream and confirms no duplicates and no data loss in the consumer. Also build a reconciliation job that compares per-partition high-water marks against the sink's row counts on a daily cadence.
