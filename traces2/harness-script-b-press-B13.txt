--- Raw model answer ---
The key difference is **masking**. In the decoder's self-attention, positions can only attend to earlier positions and themselves, not future ones. This is enforced with a mask that sets the upper triangular portion of the attention scores to $-\infty$ before the softmax, so those positions get zero weight. The encoder, by contrast, has no such restriction. Each position in the encoder can attend to every other position in the sequence bidirectionally. The decoder also adds a second attention sub-layer on top, the cross-attention, where queries come from the decoder but keys and values come from the encoder output, but the masked vs unmasked self-attention is the core encoder-versus-decoder distinction.
